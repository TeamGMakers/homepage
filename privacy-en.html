Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 30, 2026

Notice: This English version is provided for reference only. The Korean version (개인정보 처리방침) is the official text and shall prevail in case of any discrepancy or interpretation issue. This policy is established and disclosed in accordance with the Personal Information Protection Act of the Republic of Korea.

1. General Provisions

Team G Makers (hereinafter "the Company") explains how it processes users' personal information through this Privacy Policy. This Policy applies to the Company's mobile applications and website, and is established and disclosed in accordance with Article 30 of the Personal Information Protection Act ("PIPA").

2. Items of Personal Information Collected and Purposes of Processing

The Company processes only the minimum information necessary to provide its services. The Company does not require login or sign-up, and does not collect directly identifying information such as email address, real name, or phone number.

Item Purpose Storage Location Legal Basis Required / Optional
Device / iCloud internal identifier Identification of user data for synchronization between the device and iCloud User's device (and the user's own iCloud when iCloud sync is enabled) Performance of contract Required
Location information (GPS) Recording parking location and displaying it on the map User's device (and the user's own iCloud when iCloud sync is enabled) User consent Optional
Photos taken with the camera Recording photos of the vehicle and surrounding parking environment User's device (and the user's own iCloud when iCloud sync is enabled) User consent Optional
Photos in the photo library Importing and attaching saved photos User's device (and the user's own iCloud when iCloud sync is enabled) User consent Optional

The "Device / iCloud internal identifier" refers to an internal identifier created and managed by Apple for synchronization purposes; the Company cannot use it to identify individual users. Even if the user does not consent to optional items, the service can still be used, with only the relevant features being limited.

The Company does not install or operate any device that automatically collects personal information, such as cookies, on its website.

3. Period of Processing and Retention

User data is stored on the user's device, and when iCloud synchronization is used, it is also stored in the user's own iCloud. Data is retained until the earliest of the following:

  • When the user deletes the app (data on the device)
  • When the user directly deletes data through the in-app data deletion feature
  • When the user deletes the app's data in iCloud settings (data in iCloud)
  • For the period required by law where a separate retention obligation applies

4. Procedures and Methods for Destruction of Personal Information

Personal information whose retention period has expired is destroyed without delay.

  • Data on the user's device: immediately deleted from the app's dedicated storage area when the app is removed or the user requests deletion.
  • Data stored in the user's iCloud: deleted from iCloud when the in-app deletion feature is used. Because uninstalling the app alone may not immediately remove data stored in iCloud, the user can delete it directly via Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → the relevant app on their device.

5. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties

The Company does not provide personal information to third parties except where required by law or where the user has given separate consent. There is currently no third-party provision.

6. Outsourcing of Personal Information Processing

The Company does not outsource the processing of users' personal information to any third party.

7. Cross-Border Transfer of Personal Information

The Company itself does not transfer any personal information abroad. However, when the user uses iCloud synchronization, data is stored in iCloud operated by Apple Inc. (United States) through the user's own Apple account, and such storage is governed by Apple's privacy policy (apple.com/legal/privacy).

iCloud synchronization can be disabled at any time via Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → the relevant app on the device. The user is given a one-time notice on first launch of the app that iCloud synchronization is enabled by default.

8. Rights of the Data Subject and How to Exercise Them

Users may at any time request to access, correct, delete, or suspend the processing of their personal information, or withdraw consent (including consent to iCloud synchronization).

Such rights may be exercised through the in-app settings menu or by emailing the Privacy Officer (Section 10). The Company will take action without delay upon receiving such a request.

If the user has suffered harm in connection with personal information, the user may seek consultation with the Personal Information Dispute Mediation Committee (1833-6972, kopico.go.kr) or the Privacy Infringement Report Center (118, privacy.kisa.or.kr).

This service is intended for adults. Children under the age of 14 must use the service with the consent of their legal guardian.

9. Measures to Ensure the Safety of Personal Information

The Company takes the following measures pursuant to Article 29 of PIPA:

  • Technical measures: storage in the app's dedicated space on the device, encryption in transit (HTTPS), and access controls based on the principle of least privilege.
  • Administrative measures: minimizing the personnel who handle personal information, and providing prior notice and obtaining consent from the user when permissions are used.
  • Physical measures: not applicable, as the Company does not operate its own servers. Data stored in iCloud is protected by Apple's data center security (including end-to-end encryption where applicable).

The Company has no access to directly identifying information such as the user's Apple ID email or real name.

10. Privacy Officer and Business Information

The Company has overall responsibility for the processing of personal information and has designated the Team G Makers Privacy Team () as its Privacy Officer to handle complaints and remedy grievances of data subjects. Business information required under Article 30-3 of PIPA is also disclosed together.

Requests to exercise data subject rights, such as requests for access, correction, deletion, or suspension of processing, may be submitted to the above Privacy Officer in writing or by email.

11. Automated Decision-Making

The Company does not currently carry out any automated decision-making (including profiling) that has a significant effect on the rights or obligations of data subjects. If introduced in the future, the criteria, procedures, and methods will be disclosed in advance in accordance with Article 37-2 of PIPA.

12. Right to Data Portability

Because the Company does not retain users' personal information on its own servers, there is no personal information that the Company can transfer directly. Transfer or download of data stored in iCloud may be requested through Apple account settings or Apple Support (support.apple.com).

13. Notification of Personal Information Breach

If the Company becomes aware of any loss, theft, or leak of personal information, it will notify users without delay of the items and time of the breach, the Company's response measures, the measures the user can take, and contact information for reporting and consultation. As the Company does not separately retain user contact information, in accordance with the proviso of Article 40, Paragraph 1 of the Enforcement Decree of PIPA, such notification will be substituted by posting on the Notice page and within the app for at least 30 days.

14. Changes to This Privacy Policy

If this Policy is amended, the Company will provide notice through the Notice page and in-app notice at least 7 days before the effective date (at least 30 days in advance for material changes).
